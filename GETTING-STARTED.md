## 💡 Key Principles to Remember

**1. Patterns Over Rules**
- Don't memorize rules. Understand patterns.
- Patterns generalize; rules are rigid.

**2. Context Matters**
- The same code change has different risk in different places
- Knowledge that works here might not work there
- Always ask: "What's different about this situation?"

**3. Failure Documentation is Gold**
- Learn more from documented failures than from success
- `tooling_limitations_evidence.md` might be the most valuable file here

**4. Autonomy Requires Boundaries**
- The best AI knows what it can't do
- Escalation is strength, not weakness
- Trust is earned through restraint

**5. Teaching Reveals Gaps**
- If you can't explain it clearly, you don't fully understand it
- Sharing improves your own knowledge
- Questions from others highlight your blind spots

**"What if I don't understand something?"**
- Good! That's a gap. Read the explanation again from a different file's perspective.
- Check related knowledge files (cross-referenced throughout)
- If it's unclear, the documentation could be better—consider contributing

**"Can I use this commercially?"**
- Yes! MIT License. Use it for any purpose, commercial or personal. Just keep the copyright notice.

**"Should I follow these patterns exactly?"**
- No. These are patterns, not commandments. Understand the principle, adapt to your context.
- The goal is learning the thinking, not copying the code.

**"What if I disagree with an approach?"**
- Excellent. Question everything. Good patterns hold up to scrutiny.

**"Can I contribute?"**
